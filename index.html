Research

I am a PhD student at USC interested in using systems and control theory to understand the human brain. This understanding can then be applied to create a brain-machine interface, a direct communication link between a brain and a computer. Such interfaces are both the first step toward revolutionizing the way we interact with computers, as well as an incredible tool to gain insight into how the brain perceives and responds to the world.

My current research involves modeling human attention, and the brain's attentional response to multisensory inputs.
